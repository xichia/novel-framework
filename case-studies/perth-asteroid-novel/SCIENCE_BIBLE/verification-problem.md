## The Central Problem

Real asteroid detection is open, decentralized, and designed to be impossible to fake at scale.

The global planetary defense network involves:
- Dozens of survey telescopes running continuous sky surveys
- Hundreds of follow-up observatories worldwide
- Amateur astronomers with serious equipment
- Public databases (JPL Horizons, Minor Planet Center) where orbital data is freely accessible
- International bodies: NASA PDCO, ESA NEOCC, IAWN, SMPAG
- A culture of independent verification built precisely to prevent single-point failure

A conspiracy that simply *invented* an asteroid would collapse within days. Independent observers would fail to find it. The data would not match. The internet would notice.

## The Believable Window: Institutional Chokepoints

The conspiracy does not invent an asteroid from nothing. It exploits a real object — and the narrow institutional passage before independent verification locks in.

### The Discovery Pipeline

**Step 1 — Initial Detection**
Survey telescopes (Catalina Sky Survey, Pan-STARRS, ATLAS, Spacewatch) scan the sky nightly and flag moving objects. The Minor Planet Center (MPC) in Cambridge, Massachusetts, is the international clearinghouse for all new detections. New objects are posted publicly — but with high uncertainty.

**Step 2 — Short Observational Arc**
A newly detected object has been watched for only a few nights or weeks. With a short arc, orbital predictions are wildly uncertain. An object that appears threatening with three nights of data may prove entirely harmless with thirty. This uncertainty window is where the conspiracy operates.

**Step 3 — Follow-Up Observations**
The MPC issues alerts. Other observatories try to re-acquire the object. This is the critical chokepoint: if the conspiracy can *influence which observatories make follow-up observations and what they report*, it can extend and shape the uncertainty window.

**Step 4 — Orbital Refinement**
As observations accumulate, orbital models tighten. The impact probability either rises toward certainty or collapses toward zero. The conspiracy needs the probability to stay high — or to be locked in before it collapses.

**Step 5 — Risk Assessment and Escalation**
NASA's Sentry system and ESA's Meerkat system continuously monitor impact probabilities. Above certain thresholds, classified government briefings begin. This is where Giacob's expertise sits: he understands exactly when uncertainty becomes a warning that governments act on.

**Step 6 — International Notification**
IAWN (International Asteroid Warning Network) and SMPAG (Space Mission Planning Advisory Group) coordinate international response. The conspiracy needs enough institutional buy-in at this level to make the warning credible.

**Step 7 — Public Announcement**
The world is told. At this point, independent verification becomes impossible to suppress — but by now, the institutional momentum is self-sustaining.

## Where the Conspiracy Intervenes

The conspiracy does not hack telescopes or fabricate data from scratch.

It exploits a *real object* — a genuine near-Earth asteroid with a genuinely uncertain orbit — and manipulates the institutional interpretation of that object's data during the short-arc uncertainty window.

Specifically:

**Corrupted follow-up data:** A small number of key follow-up observatories — perhaps five to ten worldwide, in jurisdictions where the conspiracy has institutional reach — submit slightly altered astrometric measurements. Not invented. Nudged. Enough to keep the impact probability elevated when the natural refinement of the orbit would have caused it to collapse.

**Controlled escalation:** Giacob and colleagues inside the institutional pipeline ensure that the risk assessment reaches the classified government briefing threshold before independent amateur astronomers can accumulate enough contradicting data.

**The deflection decision:** Once governments are briefed and the public announcement is made, the narrative locks. A deflection mission is authorized. The mission launches. The mission "succeeds." The asteroid — which was never going to hit — is declared neutralized.

The beauty of this mechanism: after a successful deflection, no one expects the asteroid to be findable anymore. Its orbit has been "changed." Independent observers have no way to verify that the threat was real, because the deflection removed the evidence.

## Minimum Conspiracy Scale

For this to work, the conspiracy needs:

- **3–5 key observatory contacts** who submit altered astrometric data
- **2–3 people inside the MPC or equivalent clearinghouse** who can manage the public data pipeline
- **Institutional cover** at NASA PDCO and/or ESA NEOCC level — not necessarily conspirators, but people whose professional judgment has been shaped by the conspiracy's framing
- **Government contacts** who receive the classified briefing and ensure political response
- **The deflection mission team** — this is the most complex element; a real mission, launched for a false reason

Total active conspirators: perhaps 20–40 people. A remarkably small number to reshape the world.

This small scale is important for the novel. The conspiracy is not a vast shadowy organization. It is a small group of people who understood exactly which institutional levers to pull.

## The Real Object

The conspiracy uses a genuine asteroid.

A real object, genuinely detected, with a genuinely uncertain early orbit. Perhaps one that generated brief real concern before its orbit refined and the threat probability collapsed — but the conspiracy intervenes before that collapse.

This matters for credibility: amateur astronomers worldwide can observe the object. It is real. Its early orbital uncertainty is real. The conspiracy does not need them to fail to find it — only to find it in its genuine uncertainty, before the refined orbit (which would show safety) becomes publicly established.

## The Deflection Mission

The deflection mission is real in the sense that a spacecraft is genuinely launched and genuinely reaches the object.

NASA's DART mission (2022) proved kinetic impactor technology works. ESA's Hera mission followed up. The fictional deflection mission is a credible near-future extrapolation.

The mission "succeeds" — the asteroid's orbit is changed. The world celebrates. Independent verification of the original threat becomes permanently impossible: the orbit is now different, and no one can prove what it was before the deflection.

The public myth: *We were doomed. We cooperated. We saved ourselves.*

The conspiracy's genius: the deflection mission provides the physical proof of the response without requiring proof of the original threat. The world saved itself from something — the question of whether that something was real becomes unanswerable.

## Credibility Gaps and Mitigations

**Gap:** Independent amateur astronomers could notice the discrepancy between real and reported data.
**Mitigation:** The conspiracy targets the short-arc window when genuine uncertainty is so high that amateur data cannot decisively contradict the institutional interpretation. The deflection removes the need for later verification.

**Gap:** Whistleblowers inside the conspiracy.
**Mitigation:** The small number of true conspirators, the success of the outcome, and the moral seduction of watching it work all suppress internal dissent. Giacob himself is the only one who eventually speaks — and only to one person, decades later.

**Gap:** Post-deflection forensic analysis.
**Mitigation:** After a successful deflection, the object's orbit has genuinely changed. Pre-deflection orbital data exists in public records, but interpreting it against the institutional record requires precisely the expertise Giacob has — and he is one of the few people who could do it. He leaves Martin enough to verify, but verification itself requires deep specialist knowledge.
