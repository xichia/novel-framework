# STYLE_GUIDE.md

*The tone contract. Prevents the novel drifting into thriller cliché, sentimental melodrama, vague utopia, or abstract essay.*

---

## The Register

Literary fiction. Restrained, precise, class-conscious, emotionally exact.

The prose should feel like a person thinking carefully — not performing intelligence, not simplifying for effect, not rushing.

Sentences earn their length. A long sentence is long because the thought requires it. A short sentence is short because the silence after it matters.

---

## What the Novel Sounds Like

- Kazuo Ishiguro's *The Remains of the Day* — a narrator whose blindness is visible to the reader before it is visible to him
- Richard Flanagan's *The Unknown Terrorist* — class and institutional reality in contemporary Australia, rendered without sentimentality
- Ian McEwan's *Saturday* — a single life held against large historical forces, rendered through domestic texture
- Per Petterson's *Out Stealing Horses* — grief carried quietly across time, prose that trusts silence

The novel does not sound like a thriller. It does not sound like science fiction. It does not sound like a political manifesto or a philosophical treatise. It sounds like a person telling a story he is still deciding how to tell.

---

## Perth Voice

Martin thinks in specific, grounded terms.

He notices:
- light on the river at a specific hour
- the sound a kettle makes in a quiet house
- the class markers of a room he is entering
- what is not being said in a conversation
- the exact nature of his discomfort

He does not think in abstractions. He arrives at philosophical conclusions through concrete experience.

His inner voice is dry, sometimes sardonic, capable of unexpected tenderness. He is not self-pitying. He is self-aware to a fault — which is its own kind of blindness.

## The Utopia

Never explain the utopia. Show it through friction that has been removed.

Wrong approach:
> *The city had transformed. Global cooperation had led to housing reform, universal basic services, and a new spirit of civic life.*

Right approach:
> *The woman upstairs — he had never learned her name in eleven years — knocked on his door with a plate of something she'd made too much of. She didn't wait to be invited in.*

The reader should feel the utopia before they understand it. Specific domestic details carry more weight than any policy summary.

The utopia is imperfect. People are still people. The point is not that humanity has been perfected. The point is that the floor has risen. Dignity is less rare. Cruelty is less casual.

---

## The Science

Do not interpret "avoid overly scientific explanation" as a mandate to "minimize science." The correct principle is: avoid inert exposition, but allow science to become a major living force in the novel.

*   **Science as Character:** Science is not merely static background information; it is one of the novel's major living forces. It should be accurate, vivid, intelligible, and pleasurable to understand. 
*   **Accessible Wonder and Dread:** Scientific explanation is welcome when it creates cosmic wonder, orbital dread, problem-solving pleasure, moral pressure, or character revelation. The reader should sometimes be invited to think alongside the story, experiencing the click of comprehension.
*   **Tonal Restraint:** The voice must remain restrained, literary, morally grave, and character-driven. Avoid pop-science cheerfulness, textbook lectures, and detached info-dumps that break the book's tone.
*   **Scene Pressure and Emotional Cost:** Every scientific detail must remain attached to scene pressure, character perception, ethical consequence, and emotional cost.
*   **Martin's Translation Lens (Chapter 17):** In the confession, do not hide the science. It must arrive as Martin's devastating translation of reality: his union lawyer brain translates the coordinates and data adjustments as a "death sentence with paperwork around it." The numbers are dry, but their human consequence is absolute.
*   **Institutional Texture:** When the science appears, it should have the texture of institutional bureaucracy—dry, procedural, specific—rather than dramatic exposition. Giacob's expertise should be felt in the precision of his language, and should be broken up by physical action or environmental shifts.

## What to Avoid

### Thriller clichés
- No ticking clocks (except the approaching anniversary, which is slow and civic, not explosive)
- No physical danger to Martin
- No chase, no threat, no antagonist pursuing him
- No dramatic confrontations with conspiracy members

### Sentimentality
- Martin's former partner is not a wound that bleeds on cue
- The elder is not a wise sage handing down lessons
- The utopia is not a paradise. It is a more livable world.
- Martin's happiness is not triumphant. It is quiet and specific.

### Abstraction
- The central question is philosophical but must always be anchored in concrete life
- Martin never delivers the novel's themes as dialogue
- The moral dilemma should never be summarized by a character

### Vague emotion
- "He felt sad" is never enough
- Emotion is rendered through physical sensation, specific memory, concrete action, or notable absence of feeling
- Grief in this novel is felt in the quality of light, the weight of a quiet house, the specific thing said or not said

### Easy irony
- The novel contains enormous irony but should not wink at it
- The governing ironies (the better world arrived via the class Martin most distrusted; the lie produced what his truth could not) must be felt by the reader, not pointed at

## Strand Registers

### Strand A — Present Utopia
Warm but not soft. The prose should have the settled quality of a person who has stopped bracing against the world. After the confession, a second quality enters: the cold thing inside the warm prose.

### Strand B — Martin Before and During Countdown
Colder. Sparser. The prose mirrors Martin's economy — he has learned not to invest more than he can afford to lose. As spring arrives, something loosens. The sentences should loosen with it.

### Strand C — Elder in Europe
The most precise register. Institutional, restrained, morally elegant. The rooms are specific. The language of science and bureaucracy is rendered accurately. Emotion is most deeply submerged here and most powerful when it surfaces.

---

## Class

Class is never announced. It is shown.

Martin knows which rooms he was not supposed to enter. He entered them. He felt the temperature drop. He never forgot.

In the utopia, he notices the absence of this temperature drop. It is one of the novel's quietest and most important details.

The elder, by contrast, has never noticed the temperature in rooms. He has always been the one setting it. This is his blindness. The confession is partly an admission of it.

---

## Dialogue

Spare. Exact. People do not explain themselves in dialogue.

What is not said is always present.

Martin's dialogue is direct but deflects at the emotional center. The elder's dialogue is precise and occasionally unintentionally revealing. They talk around the most important things for most of the novel.

---

## The Ending

The ending must resist:
- Resolution
- Comfort
- Summary
- A final statement of theme

The ending must offer:
- A specific image
- A precise emotional state
- A question the reader carries out of the book
- The ambiguity held rather than collapsed

The final pages should feel like the moment before a decision that may never be made.
