## PARK-001 — Alternative Ending: Martin Releases the Truth and the Utopia Survives

**Parked from:** Ending discussion

**Idea:** Martin releases the elder's evidence. The world learns the asteroid was fabricated. After shock, grief, and anger — humanity chooses to keep what it built. The utopia survives the truth.

**Why it's interesting:** The most optimistic version of the novel. Would argue that the human connection produced by the lie has become genuinely independent of its origin. The lie becomes irrelevant because what it created has its own reality now.

**Why it's parked:** Very hard to earn. Risks feeling like a wish rather than a conclusion. Would require the ending to carry enormous narrative weight. May also undermine the novel's commitment to ambiguity.

**When to revisit:** After the chapter outline is complete and the ending branches are developed.

## PARK-002 — The Elder's Personal Bereavement

**Parked from:** Elder character development

**Idea:** The elder experienced a personal loss that made him vulnerable to the conspiracy's logic — someone he loved who died in a conflict that cooperation would have prevented. His complicity then has a private emotional root, not just a philosophical one.

**Why it's interesting:** Deepens the elder from intellectual to emotional. His sin becomes not just that he wanted humanity to flourish but that he had a face for the suffering he was trying to prevent. Makes the mirror with Martin stronger — both men are partially motivated by private grief disguised as public principle.

**Why it's parked:** Risks making the elder too sympathetic in a way that softens the moral architecture. Also risks a biographical backstory feeling mechanical. Needs testing against the character before accepting.

**When to revisit:** When drafting Strand C scenes.

## PARK-003 — Living Conspiracy Members as Pressure

**Parked from:** Plot architecture discussion

**Idea:** Other members of the conspiracy are still alive and become aware that the elder confessed. They apply pressure on Martin — not threats necessarily, but presence, arguments, the human face of people who would lose everything.

**Why it's interesting:** Would introduce a second set of moral voices and complicate Martin's decision. Could give the novel a scene where Martin hears the conspiracy's best arguments from people who believed in it.

**Why it's parked:** High risk of introducing thriller mechanics. The novel's decision was to keep the pressure coming from conscience and circumstance, not from external actors. This would undermine that.

**When to revisit:** Only if the novel's present strand feels insufficiently pressurized.

## PARK-004 — Title: *Perth*

**Parked from:** Title discussion

**Idea:** The novel is simply called *Perth*. Minimal. The place as the meaning. Carries the Bon Iver resonance.

**Why it's interesting:** Stronger than it sounds. A one-word place-name title can carry enormous weight (*Amsterdam*, *Middlemarch*, *London*). *Perth* as a title would be quietly strange and specific in a way that matches the novel's register.

**Why it's parked:** *The Warm Room* accepted as working title. *Perth* remains the strongest alternative.

**When to revisit:** Late in development, when the full novel's shape is clear.

## PARK-005 — A Scientific Colleague Who Refused

**Parked from:** Secondary cast discussion

**Idea:** A European scientist who was approached for the conspiracy and refused. The elder knows this person. Perhaps they are still alive. Perhaps Martin can find them.

**Why it's interesting:** Would give the conspiracy a moral counterpoint — showing that refusal was possible, which deepens the elder's guilt. Would also give Martin a potential interlocutor outside Perth.

**Why it's parked:** Risks expanding the plot beyond the novel's intimate scope. The science bible may make this structurally necessary or structurally impossible.

**When to revisit:** During science bible development and plot architecture.

## PARK-006 — Martin's Former Partner Still in Perth

**Parked from:** Partner location discussion

**Idea:** Martin's former partner never left Perth. She is somewhere in the city. The possibility of encounter is always present.

**Why it's interesting:** Adds an unnerving proximity to Martin's partial knowledge. In a utopia where the city has opened up, running into her becomes possible in a way it wasn't when Perth was a city of performative distance. This could be a source of quiet dread and longing.

**Why it's parked:** Still active as a question (OQ-009). Needs decision before it becomes structural.

**When to revisit:** During chapter outline development.
