## Entry 001

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** Genre set as literary fiction, not thriller or political drama.

**Reason:** The premise's philosophical weight requires character-driven, slow-burn treatment. A thriller would subordinate the moral dilemma to plot mechanics. Literary fiction allows the dilemma to be the engine.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- Thriller (plot-driven, high tension)
- Political drama (institutions at center)
- Hybrid (literary depth with thriller momentum)

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:**
Hybrid was the closest contender, but the decision was made to commit fully to literary fiction. The thriller elements — the conspiracy, the evidence, the decision — work better as submerged pressure than as surface action.

**Downstream Consequences:** Prose must be restrained and precise. Plot mechanics must remain credible but never dominate. The science must hold up but not be explained exhaustively. The ending must resist resolution.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 002

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The fabricated crisis is a faked asteroid impact threat, not a supervolcano, earthquake/tsunami, or pandemic.

**Reason:** The asteroid threat provides a global countdown structure, existential dread without immediate physical destruction, a science-rich institutional architecture for the hoax, and a sacred deflection myth ("we cooperated and saved ourselves") that serves the utopia's emotional foundation.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- Supervolcano eruption
- Megathrust earthquake and tsunami
- Pandemic of natural origin

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:**
Pandemic too recent and culturally raw; also difficult to fake convincingly at a global level without physical symptoms. Supervolcano and earthquake are geographically localized and visually verifiable by too many independent observers. The asteroid threat uniquely exploits the gap between institutional data and public understanding.

**Downstream Consequences:** Science bible must address asteroid detection pipelines, observational uncertainty, institutional escalation, and deflection mission design.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 003

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The protagonist is Martin, a middle-aged working-class Australian man from Perth.

**Reason:** An ordinary person whose life is transformed by the countdown is the most humanizing lens for the premise. His class background gives the novel its political and emotional texture. Perth gives it its distinctive atmosphere.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- The scientist who built the deception
- A journalist deciding whether to publish
- One of the elite architects seeking confession

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:**
The scientist and journalist center the plot mechanics over the human experience. The architect was tempting but risks making the novel feel like an elite drama. Martin as ordinary person keeps the philosophical question grounded in lived life.

**Downstream Consequences:** Martin's interiority must carry the novel. His class consciousness must be specific and earned, not gestural. Perth must be inhabited, not scenic.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 004

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** Martin's wound is dual: political disillusionment (former union-side employment lawyer) and the loss of his long-term partner (a primary school teacher).

**Reason:** The political wound explains his cynicism about the world. The lost partner explains his isolation. Both are needed. One affects the mind; the other affects the heart. Together they make the utopia's gift to him personal and irreversible.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- Political disillusionment alone
- Personal betrayal alone
- Accumulated small disappointments

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:**
Single wounds risk making Martin one-dimensional. The dual wound structure mirrors the novel's dual question — about public truth and private mercy.

**Downstream Consequences:** Martin's lost partner must be a fully realized character, not merely a wound. His legal career must be specifically rendered. The case that broke him needs to be designed.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 005

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The primary setting is Perth, Western Australia. The European strand is set in Geneva and a private rural estate.

**Reason:** Perth is the most isolated major city on earth. Its isolation mirrors Martin's emotional condition. Its specific seasonal character — bleak winters, golden late summers, vast sky, Indian Ocean light — provides the novel's atmospheric architecture. It is almost entirely absent from this kind of literary fiction, which is itself a reason to be there.

**Alternatives Considered:** Other Australian cities; European cities for the whole novel; no fixed setting.

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:** Sydney or Melbourne lack Perth's geographic and emotional isolation. European literary settings are overcrowded. The contrast between Perth's edge-of-the-world position and the global conspiracy centered in Geneva is structurally meaningful.

**Downstream Consequences:** Must research Perth's outer-suburb class geography, seasonal light, civic life, winter quality, and specific neighborhoods. The utopia must be rendered in Perth's specific texture.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 006

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** Three-strand braided structure: present utopia (Strand A), Martin before and during countdown (Strand B), elder in Europe (Strand C).

**Reason:** The three timelines allow the novel to work simultaneously as present drama, emotional backstory, and moral archaeology. The reader inhabits Martin's happiness while understanding how it was constructed and what it cost.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- Linear chronology
- Two strands only (present and past)
- Non-linear but single-perspective

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:** Linear chronology delays the utopia and reduces dramatic irony. Two strands omit the elder's European world, which is essential for the science credibility and moral complexity of the hoax.

**Downstream Consequences:** Chapter planning must manage three rhythms. Transitions between strands must be earned. The European strand needs its own prose register — more precise and restrained than the Perth strands.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 007

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The elder is a semi-retired European astrophysicist and UN-adjacent planetary-risk strategist. He was not the mastermind but the dramaturg — the expert who gave the false crisis institutional reality.

**Reason:** This role makes him complicit without making him villainous. His sin is expertise, not malice. His arc (consultant → strategist → reluctant accomplice → haunted witness) is morally complex and mirrors Martin's arc in structure if not content.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- The mastermind himself
- A peripheral functionary
- A political operative

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:** The mastermind would be too powerful and too distant from Martin's register. A peripheral functionary wouldn't have enough moral weight or self-knowledge. A political operative would shift the novel's tone toward political thriller.

**Downstream Consequences:** The elder must understand institutional asteroid science in granular and believable detail. His European backstory must be rendered with precision. His friendship with Martin must be genuine and not merely instrumental.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 008

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The elder finds Martin by accident and recognizes something in him organically, not by design.

**Reason:** A deliberately chosen confessor would make the elder manipulative and the friendship transactional. The accidental meeting preserves the authenticity of the relationship. What he recognizes — the hollow shape of abandoned belief — is something he carries himself.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- The elder sought Martin out deliberately for a specific reason
- The elder chose Martin because of his ordinariness as a representative of those deceived

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:** Both alternatives reduce the elder's final moral act — the refusal to control the outcome — to strategy. The accidental meeting makes his eventual trust in Martin feel genuinely chosen.

**Downstream Consequences:** The meeting scene must be specific and unforced. The friendship must develop through ordinary things before any confession.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 009

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The elder gives Martin evidence and no instructions. No ticking clock. No automatic release mechanism.

**Reason:** Any forced deadline transforms the novel into a thriller. The pressure must come from conscience and circumstance — specifically, from the world praising a story Martin knows to be false, and his silence becoming more active every day.

**Downstream Consequences:** The external pressure mechanism needs design. The approaching anniversary of the deflection is the current candidate. Martin may be invited to speak at a civic ceremony.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 010

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** Seasonal architecture established. Winter = before. Spring = countdown. Late summer/early autumn = utopia. Deepening autumn into early winter = hygge.

**Reason:** The seasons externalize Martin's emotional arc without forcing metaphor. The hygge quality of Perth's autumn-into-winter — cold outside, warmth inside, people staying rather than leaving — is the specific texture of what the utopia gave him that nothing before ever did.

**Downstream Consequences:** Every scene must be seasonally grounded. Chapter planning must map scenes to seasons precisely.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 011

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** The ending does not resolve the central question. Martin likely does not destroy the evidence and does not release it immediately. He creates a second handoff — a sealed archive or entrusted record for a future moment.

**Reason:** Resolution would betray the novel's philosophical commitment to ambiguity. The second handoff mirrors the elder's act while distinguishing it: the elder handed off for release; Martin hands off because truth without timing can become violence. The ending should preserve the question of whether Martin's distinction is wisdom or self-deception.

**Downstream Consequences:** The final image is Martin in a warm room, carrying the cold secret. The prose ending must resist sentimentality and easy comfort.

**Status:** Canon

## Entry 012

**Version:** v0.1

**Decision:** Working title is *The Warm Room*.

**Reason:** The title carries the novel's central image without explaining it. It is intimate and domestic, which is the correct register for a novel about global deception experienced through private life.

**Alternatives Considered:**
- *The Deflection* (scientific and metaphorical but cooler)
- *Perth* (minimal and resonant but possibly too opaque)
- *The Dramaturg* (unusual and precise but narrower)
- *What We Were Told* (morally weighted but slightly accusatory)

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:** *Perth* was the strongest alternative. May be revisited. *The Warm Room* was chosen because it is immediately felt before it is understood — which is how the novel should work.

**Status:** Canon (working title — may be revisited)

## Entry 013

**Version:** v0.2

**Decision:** The elder's name is Giacob. Martin's former partner's name is Bianca.

**Reason:** Giacob — Eastern European in resonance, institutional, quietly authoritative. Carries the weight of a man who has inhabited serious rooms across decades. Sits between nationalities in a way that suits someone who has spent his career in international spaces. Bianca — warm, bright, specifically Australian without cliché. A name with daily warmth that suits her embodied, practical hope.

**Alternatives Considered:** Laszlo, Edmund, Heinrich, François (elder); Clare, Helen, Ruth, Diane (partner).

**Why Alternatives Were Rejected:** Giacob has an unusual precision — not quite any one country — that matches the elder's character. Bianca was the unanimous choice.

**Downstream Consequences:** Update all character files and canon references. Close OQ-001 and OQ-002.

**Status:** Canon
