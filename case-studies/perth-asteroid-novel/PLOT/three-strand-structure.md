## Strand A — Present Utopia

Martin in Perth, late summer into autumn.

He is genuinely happy. He belongs somewhere. He has people.

The elder appears. Their friendship develops across weeks of autumn evenings — walks, meals, books, dry conversation.

The confession arrives. Martin inherits the truth.

The anniversary of the deflection approaches. His silence becomes more active every day.

The novel ends here — in the turn toward winter, hygge deepening, Martin deciding or failing to decide.

---

## Strand B — Martin Before and During the Countdown

Perth in winter and spring.

Martin alone. The retreat. The smaller life. The specific gravity of the house after she left.

The asteroid announcement. His resistance. His dry certainty that this too will pass, that people will not change, that the feeling will evaporate.

The slow cracking. A neighbor. A conversation. The specific small moment that shouldn't have mattered and did.

Spring arriving. Martin not quite the same person winter left.

---

## Strand C — Elder in Europe

Geneva and the estate. Decades earlier.

The elder at the edge of the conspiracy. The preparedness exercise that becomes real. His expertise deployed step by step.

The argument he makes to himself. The first sign it is working. The decision to stay silent permanently.

His haunted witness years. Watching the world he helped build.

The decision to go to Perth. The plane.

---

## Interweaving Principle

Strand A is the primary strand. The reader lives here.

Strands B and C illuminate Strand A from below — like light through water.

The strands should echo each other structurally: scenes in one strand should rhyme with scenes in another without being mechanically parallel.

The European strand has the most distinct prose register. When it appears, the reader should feel the shift in temperature.
