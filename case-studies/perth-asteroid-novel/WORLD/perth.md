## Why Perth

Perth is the most isolated major city on earth.

Closer to Singapore than to Sydney. Surrounded by the Indian Ocean to the west and the world's oldest and most inhospitable desert to the east. A city that has spent its entire existence at a remove from everywhere else.

This isolation is not merely geographic. It has produced a particular temperament: self-sufficient, slightly suspicious of the east coast, accustomed to enduring distance. Perth people have a complicated relationship with isolation — they have learned to inhabit it without always knowing that is what they are doing.

It is exactly the right city for Martin. And exactly the right city to show what the utopia changed.

## Class Geography

Perth has specific class geography that the novel must use precisely.

**The western suburbs** (Cottesloe, Claremont, Nedlands, Subiaco): Old money and professional class. Tree-lined, close to the ocean, expensive. The rooms Martin was not supposed to enter.

**The inner suburbs** (Leederville, Mount Lawley, Northbridge, Fremantle): Cultural and professional middle class. Cafés, art, politics, the language of the left. Where Martin would have circled in his activist years.

**The outer suburbs** (Midland, Armadale, Rockingham, Balga): Working class. Where Martin came from. Fibro and brick veneer, long commutes, TABs, community leagues. The landscape his legal career tried to represent.

**The industrial north** (Osborne Park, Malaga, Wangara): Where working Perth actually works.

Martin's internal class compass is always on. He knows where he is in every room.

## Seasonal Texture

### Winter (June–August)

Perth winters are a surprise to people who imagine Australia as uniformly warm.

Grey skies. Persistent rain. A cold that is not extreme but is relentless. The light flattens. The Indian Ocean stops being inviting and becomes indifferent. The city turns inward.

Before the utopia: Martin's natural habitat. The winter did not oppress him — it confirmed him. It was easier to be alone when the city was also alone.

The Bon Iver song *Perth* opens in this register: something still, something cold, something about the quality of waiting.

### Spring (September–October)

Perth springs can be spectacular — wildflowers across the south-west, sudden warmth, the city exhaling after winter.

During the countdown: the world cracking open. People appearing. Martin resisting the feeling that it means something.

### Summer (November–February)

Harsh. Bleaching. The sun is not gentle here. The east wind brings heat from the interior. Fires. The city retreats to air conditioning and the ocean.

The utopia in summer is recognizable as Perth but less performatively isolated — people linger in the evenings when the heat breaks, which it usually does.

### Late Summer / Autumn (March–May)

The novel's present tense.

This is Perth at its most beautiful and most livable. The ferocity of summer softens. The light turns amber and golden. Evenings lengthen in a different way — not toward heat but toward that particular late-afternoon quality where no one is in a hurry. The ocean is warm enough to swim in without the punishing sun.

As autumn deepens toward winter, the hygge quality emerges: candles, warm rooms, the cold outside making the inside matter. Perth winters are cold enough for this to feel earned.

## Specific Perth Locations

### The Swan River

The river is central to Perth's civic geography. The foreshore around the CBD and through Nedlands and Crawley is where people walk, sit, watch. It is where Martin and the elder likely meet and where they walk together.

In the utopia: the river foreshore has more life. People who used to jog past each other now stop.

### The Indian Ocean Beaches

Cottesloe, Scarborough, City Beach, Trigg. These beaches are among the most beautiful in the world and are fully known only to people who live there.

Martin's habit: driving to the ocean and sitting with it. This is Perth's specific form of solitude.

### The State Library of Western Australia

On James Street, Perth CBD. A building with civic dignity. A place Martin may work in the post-collapse years. A natural setting for the elder to visit — he is drawn to libraries everywhere.

### Fremantle

Perth's port city, fifteen minutes south. More working class and bohemian than Perth proper. A distinct atmosphere. May be relevant to Martin's background or to the utopia's texture.

## The Utopia in Perth

The utopia does not make Perth unrecognizable. It makes Perth more itself.

The isolation was always a performance as much as a condition. The distance was real; the coldness between people was partly chosen and partly learned.

What changes:

- People who used to nod now stop
- Doors that used to stay closed open
- The community hall or library that used to be half-empty is now full
- Working-class people in the outer suburbs experience a specific material relief — housing, services, the absence of the grinding anxiety of precarity
- The city's class geography softens without disappearing
- The Indian Ocean, seen from the beach on a warm autumn evening with other people, feels different than the same ocean seen alone

What does not change:

- The light
- The distance
- The particular dry humour
- The suspicion of performance and pretension
- Perth's complicated relationship with the rest of Australia
- The winter

## Perth and the Novel's Themes

Perth is the perfect setting for this novel because:

1. Its isolation mirrors Martin's isolation — and their simultaneous opening is the novel's emotional arc.

2. Its distance from global centers of power (Geneva, New York, Brussels) makes it the ideal place to see what the conspiracy produced at the furthest remove from where it was constructed.

3. Its specific class geography gives Martin's story material grounding.

4. Its natural beauty — the ocean, the sky, the light — earns the utopia's warmth without requiring it to be explained.

5. It is almost entirely absent from literary fiction of this kind, which is itself meaningful. The novel brings the edge of the world to the center of a moral question.
