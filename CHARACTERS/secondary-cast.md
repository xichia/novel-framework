# CHARACTERS/secondary-cast.md

*Secondary characters are not yet canon. This file is active development territory.*

---

## Purpose of Secondary Characters

Secondary characters in the utopia do two things:

1. They embody the utopia's daily texture — they are the warmth Martin has found.
2. At least one of them would be devastated by the truth, deepening Martin's moral dilemma.

Secondary characters in Strand B (pre-utopia and countdown) show the world Martin retreated from and the early warmth that began to crack his isolation.

Secondary characters in Strand C (Europe) give the conspiracy a human texture beyond the elder alone.

---

## Present Utopia (Strand A)

### The Neighbor — TBD

A neighbor who became something more. Probably the person who first arrived with food, without invitation, early in the utopia. The woman upstairs, or next door, who knocked.

In the before-years, Martin may have nodded to her in the way Perth people nod — acknowledging presence without risking connection. Now they share meals.

She should be:
- Working class or lower-middle class — someone whose life the utopia improved concretely
- Warm without being sentimental
- Someone with her own history and her own losses
- Someone who would be genuinely shattered if the utopia's foundation were revealed

She is one reason Martin cannot simply release the evidence. He sees her face and he cannot.

**Status:** Needs development. Name and biography TBD.

---

### The Community — TBD

A small constellation of people Martin now belongs to. Not a defined group — more a loose civic warmth. People from the neighborhood, from wherever he now works, from the library or community spaces.

These people do not need individual development at this stage. They exist as texture: the long table outside at dusk, the shared meals, the specific quality of people who have learned to linger.

**Status:** Texture only at this stage.

## Countdown / Before (Strand B)

### The First Person Who Broke Through — TBD

Someone who reached Martin during the countdown before he was ready to be reached. Probably not a grand gesture — something small and unexpected. A conversation in a queue. A neighbor doing something impractical and kind. A stranger on the foreshore.

This character may or may not appear in Strand A (present). If they appear in both, they become a bridge between Martin's old and new self.

**Status:** Needs development.

---

### Martin's Colleague or Former Colleague — TBD

Someone from his legal career who knew him at his best — or at the moment of collapse. A younger lawyer he mentored, perhaps. Or a union organizer who understood what the compromise cost him.

May appear in Strand B only, or may resurface in Strand A as someone the utopia has also transformed.

**Status:** Parked.

## European Strand (Strand C)

### The Mastermind(s) — TBD

The people who conceived the conspiracy. The elder is not among them — he was brought in. These figures exist in Strand C at a remove: seen across tables, named in documents, felt as a presence.

They should not be rendered as villains. They should be rendered as people with comprehensible logic who made a catastrophic presumption. The banality of their conference rooms is important — the conspiracy should look like the ordinary furniture of institutional power, not like something from a thriller.

**Status:** Structural necessity. Development deferred to science bible and plot architecture.

---

### A Scientific Colleague of the Elder — TBD

Someone the elder worked with who knew what was happening. Perhaps someone who also fell. Perhaps someone who refused and was sidelined. A relationship that shows the elder's complicity from outside his own perspective.

**Status:** Parked — possibly very useful for Strand C texture.
