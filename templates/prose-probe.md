---

## Purpose

A prose probe tests tone, register, and voice for a single scene before a full chapter packet or draft is written. It is the cheapest way to discover whether an architectural idea works at sentence level. It is not a draft. It should take 300–1,200 words.

## Inputs to Read

- `CANON.md` — relevant canon facts for the scene
- `STYLE_GUIDE.md` — tone, register, and voice constraints
- `THEMES.md` — thematic architecture the scene must serve
- `OPEN_QUESTIONS.md` — unresolved decisions affecting the scene
- Any relevant architecture artifact or stress test

## Constraints

- Maximum word count: 1,200 words. If the probe exceeds this, it is testing too much.
- The probe tests one dramatic moment only. It does not test the full chapter arc.
- The probe may invent staging details (props, physical actions, sensory details) but must mark them as provisional.
- The probe must not introduce new canon facts. All invented specifics are provisional.
- The probe must carry a non-canon notice at the top.
