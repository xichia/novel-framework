# CANON.md

*The source of truth. No maybes. No alternatives. No brainstorms. Only accepted story facts.*

---

## Premise

A secret elite group fabricated an asteroid impact threat to force humanity into cooperation.

The countdown worked. Humanity united. Conflicts paused. Borders softened. Resources were redistributed. Civic life deepened. Ordinary people began caring for one another.

The asteroid was false.

The better world is real.

---

## Central Question

*If a lie produces genuine human connection, is the connection real?*

Later refined as:

*When is not knowing an act of mercy, and when is it cowardice?*

---

## Genre and Tone

Literary fiction. Character-driven, morally complex, slow-burn, intimate, philosophical, and grounded in credible science. Not a conspiracy thriller. The conspiracy is context; the drama is ethical and emotional.

---

## Characters

### Martin

- Middle-aged working-class man from Perth, Western Australia.
- Grew up in the outer suburbs — Midland, Armadale, or similar. A landscape of Hungry Jack's and TABs and fathers who work with their hands.
- Gifted and intelligent. Noticed by teachers. Went further than expected. Felt the temperature drop in rooms he was not supposed to enter.
- Became a union-side employment lawyer. Represented workers, unions, low-paid people, those exploited by systems designed to exhaust them.
- His professional gift: making suffering legible to institutions.
- His flaw: he gave himself to justice in the abstract and failed love in the particular.
- Lost his partner because he kept choosing the cause over their life together.
- Lost his job after a major case ended in institutional betrayal. He was asked to sell a compromise to workers who trusted him. He refused — or he sold it and became what he despised. Either way, the job was gone.
- Retreated into a smaller life. Possible role: library assistant, council worker, civic admin — beneath his abilities but still connected to public life.
- In the utopia: genuinely happy for the first time. He belongs somewhere. He has people. He has been seen without having to fight to be seen.
- His isolation before the utopia was not just circumstance. It was a worldview made physical.

### Martin's Former Partner

- A primary school teacher.
- Warm, practical, daily in her hope. Not less political than Martin — her politics were smaller in scale but perhaps truer.
- She spent every day with actual children. Feed the child. Notice the child. Remember what they said yesterday. Make one frightened person feel safe.
- Left Martin because staying meant becoming secondary forever to a future that never arrived.
- Martin loved her enough to let her leave, but not enough to become someone who could ask her to stay.
- Her name: Bianca
- In the utopia: Martin eventually searches for her. He finds enough to know she is alive — a school newsletter, staff page, community project, or photo with children. He stops before learning whether she is married, whether she has children, whether she is happy, whether she ever thinks of him.
- This mirrors the novel's central question: with her, Martin chooses not to know everything because knowing might hurt him. With the world, he must decide whether that same mercy is wisdom or cowardice.

### The Elder

- An elderly European man. Not famous. Deeply respected in serious institutional rooms.
- Semi-retired European astrophysicist and UN-adjacent planetary-risk strategist.
- His specific expertise: near-Earth-object risk, institutional verification, scientific uncertainty, and crisis communication.
- He understands how astronomical uncertainty becomes institutional certainty — how observatories verify objects, how threat probabilities are escalated, when governments act, how scientists communicate risk, how to make a false crisis feel discovered rather than announced.
- He was not the original mastermind of the hoax. He was brought in as a consultant, possibly for a theoretical preparedness exercise.
- Gradually he realized the machinery was manufacturing a crisis, not merely planning for one.
- His tragic arc: consultant → strategist → reluctant accomplice → haunted witness.
- He did not create the lie. He gave it procedural reality. He is not the face of the theatre. He is the dramaturg.
- His sin is expertise. He understood the system so well that he could make the false crisis institutionally believable.
- He fell not for greed, fame, or vanity. He fell because the lie worked. He watched humanity cooperate and could not bring himself to stop it.
- His name: Giacob
- He comes to Perth in the present narrative because he wants to see whether the lie worked somewhere ordinary — at the edge of the world, as far from the centers of power as a city can be.
- He meets Martin by accident and recognizes something: a man who once believed and now carries the hollow shape of abandoned belief.
- Their friendship is real before the confession.
- He eventually gives Martin the truth: a recorded confession, written account, technical documents, institutional correspondence, names, and enough material for Martin to verify the fabrication.
- He does not give Martin a deadline or an automatic release mechanism.
- His final respect for Martin: he refuses to make one more elite decision on behalf of someone else. He hands over the truth and does not tell Martin what to do.

### Martin and the Elder as Mirrors

Both are variations of the same moral failure.

- Martin sacrificed private love for public justice.
- The elder sacrificed public truth for public peace.
- Both committed their sins through their virtues.
- Martin's virtue: he cannot ignore suffering.
- The elder's virtue: he cannot ignore the possibility of saving humanity.
- Both translated reality into forms institutions could use. Martin translated worker suffering into legal cases. The elder translated astronomical uncertainty into institutional action.
- Both learn that translation is never innocent.

## Setting

**Perth, Western Australia.**

Perth matters because of:

- isolation — the most isolated major city on earth
- Indian Ocean light
- vast sky
- outer-suburban class geography
- winter bleakness and late-summer/autumn beauty
- the contrast between solitude and civic warmth
- the specific Australian quality of a place that has learned to endure distance

The European strand is set primarily in **Geneva** (institutional, precise, the language of global governance) and **a private estate** (possibly rural France or northern Switzerland — stone, land, fireside, the place where the real conversations happen).

## Seasonal Architecture

| Period | Season | Quality |
|---|---|---|
| Before countdown | Perth winter | Grey, wet, cold, solitary. Martin's loneliness externalized. |
| Countdown | Late winter into spring | The world cracks open. People begin turning toward each other. Martin resists, then slowly thaws. |
| Present utopia | Late summer into autumn | Golden, settled, cooling, communal. Not paradise — ripening. |
| Autumn deepening | Moving toward winter | Hygge. Shared warmth. The cold outside makes the inside matter. |

Key principle: **The utopia did not change the weather. It changed whether people endured the weather alone.**

## Utopia Texture

Shown through daily life, not policy lectures.

- Neighbors arriving without invitation
- Shared meals
- Long tables outside at dusk
- People lingering instead of leaving
- Children moving safely through the city
- Libraries and civic spaces full of life
- Working-class people treated with dignity
- Fewer invisible hierarchies
- The city no longer performing isolation

Concrete political changes exist beneath the texture:

- Demilitarization
- Softened borders
- Debt relief
- Housing reform
- Universal basic services
- Climate mobilization
- Shorter work weeks
- Stronger local civic life

## Structure

Three braided strands.

**Strand A — Present Utopia**
Martin in Perth, late summer into autumn. He is genuinely happy. The elder appears. Friendship develops. The confession arrives. Martin inherits the truth. He must decide what to do. This is the primary strand and the longest.

**Strand B — Martin Before and During Countdown**
Perth in winter and spring. Martin's life before: the partner, the job, the retreat. The asteroid announcement. His resistance to collective feeling. The slow thaw. The beginning of community. The first warmth.

**Strand C — Elder in Europe**
The elder's past. Geneva rooms. The private estate. The preparedness exercise that became a conspiracy. His expertise deployed step by step. The moment he understood what he had become. The decision to continue anyway. This strand ends on a plane to Perth.

## Science Principle

The asteroid hoax must be scientifically and institutionally credible.

The believable version: the conspiracy exploits the narrow window before certainty — the passage from ambiguous astronomical data to institutional warning.

The elder's expertise sits exactly there. He understands discovery pipelines, observational uncertainty, follow-up verification, institutional escalation, crisis communication, and how official warnings become believable.

The public origin myth involves a successful deflection mission.

Emotional myth: *We were doomed. We cooperated. We saved ourselves.*

This becomes the sacred origin story of the utopia.

---

## Ending Direction

Martin likely does not destroy the evidence.

He likely does not release it immediately.

He may create a second handoff: a sealed archive, legal testimony, future record, or entrusted document.

Key distinction:
- The elder hands off because he wants release.
- Martin hands off because he has learned that truth without timing can become violence.

Or maybe that is what Martin tells himself.

The ending should preserve that ambiguity. It should sharpen the question, not answer it.

---

## Governing Ironies

- Martin sacrificed love for justice and lost both. The architects sacrificed truth for peace and gained both — for a while.
- Martin once withheld knowledge from himself about the woman he loved. Now he must decide whether withholding knowledge from the world is mercy or cowardice.
- The better world Martin fought for arrived only after the world was deceived by the class of people he distrusted most.

---

## Core Final Image

Martin in a warm room in Perth — autumn or early winter. Friends, food, children, candlelight, rain or cool air outside. The world around him is gentle, communal, alive.

He is surrounded by the kind of human connection he once believed impossible.

Inside him is the elder's secret.

**The secret is the cold thing he holds inside the warm room.**
